# Voice and microcopy

Read this before writing any copy that ships on the site, in a press response, or in a records request. The brief's tone constraints are tight on purpose: the coalition's credibility comes from procedural literacy, not from rhetoric.

## Headlines

- Declarative. State what is true.
- No exclamation points. Anywhere.
- No em dashes. Use a period or a comma.
- No loaded adjectives. "Massive," "shocking," "unprecedented" are out. "266-acre," "second-reading," "first-quarter" are in.
- Cite numbers when you have them. Ordinance numbers, dates, gallons, acres, parcel IDs.

Good: *A neighborhood reading the ordinance, line by line.*
Good: *Public records. Public hearings. Public land.*
Good: *Read the ordinance. Then decide.*
Bad: *Stop the data center now!*
Bad: *Massive industrial complex threatens our community*

## Buttons

Verbs in sentence case. End with a period when the button stands as a complete sentence.

- *Read the ordinance.*
- *File a records request.*
- *See the timeline.*
- *Open PDF →*

Use the right arrow `→` (literal Unicode arrow, mono-typeset) for forward navigation. Do not use `>` or `>>`.

## Empty states

Name what is missing and what to do next. Two sentences, max.

Good: *No hearing scheduled yet. Check back, or subscribe for updates.*
Good: *No documents filed for this parcel. The next planning packet is expected on April 8.*
Bad: *Nothing to see here.*
Bad: *Coming soon!*

## Numerical formatting

Numbers carry the brand's authority. Typeset them like evidence.

- Ordinance, parcel, and request IDs in IBM Plex Mono: `ORD 2025-23`, `FXF-PR-2026-001`, `266.971 ac`.
- Dates as `MM.DD` over the year: `04.08 / 2026`. (Component already does this; do not retype.)
- Gallons and acres get a unit, no comma decoration: `1,250,000 gal/day`, `266.971 ac`.
- Tabular numerals on every table cell, every timeline date, every doc card date.
- Never italicize numbers for emphasis.

## Acceptable taglines

These have been cleared for use anywhere — masthead, social, signoff:

- Public records. Public hearings. Public land.
- Read the ordinance. Then decide.
- Civic. Procedural. Rooted in place.

If you want a new one, run it through the do/don't list before using it. New taglines should not appear in the cover masthead — alternates are footer- or social-only.

## Things never to write

- Anything that reads like a yard sign ("Stop K-Nova!", "Save Foxfire!", "Vote No!").
- Anything alarmist or apocalyptic ("ominous," "looming," "threat," "destroy").
- Anything conspiratorial ("rigged," "behind closed doors," "they don't want you to know").
- Anything partisan or party-coded ("liberal," "conservative," "Republican," "Democrat").
- Anything snarky, sarcastic, or aggrieved.
- Anti-technology language ("the data center monster," "Big Tech," "Silicon Valley").
- Loaded adjectives ("massive," "shocking," "unprecedented," "horrific").

If a sentence sounds like it could appear in a campaign mailer or a tech-startup landing page, rewrite it.

## When in doubt

The voice is **factual, procedural, calm, specific**. If you are uncertain whether a line clears the bar, drop it. The coalition has time. We are tracking every filing, every hearing, and every gallon — in public, on the record.
