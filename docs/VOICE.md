# Voice and microcopy

Read this before writing any copy that ships on the site, in a press response, or in a records request. The voice is hybrid by design: procedural in the body, direct in the hero and the CTAs. Both registers are bound by the same hard rules.

## The two registers

**Body register.** Factual, procedural, calm, specific. Used for explainers, timeline entries, document descriptions, FAQ answers, sources blocks, and any prose that asserts a claim. Cite ordinance numbers, dates, gallons, acres, parcel IDs. Avoid editorial adjectives.

**Hero and CTA register.** Direct. Plain words. Short sentences. Allowed to be firm. Used for the homepage hero, page-level kickers and calls to action, the signup form prompt, the action buttons. Still bound by the hard rules below.

A page that opens with a strong direct hero in the hero register transitions into the body register the moment it starts asserting facts. The body never imitates a campaign mailer.

## Hard rules that apply to both registers

These are the bright lines. They do not change.

- No em dashes. Use a period, a comma, or a colon.
- No exclamation points anywhere on the site or in any printed material.
- No conspiratorial language ("rigged," "behind closed doors," "secret deal," "they don't want you to know").
- No partisan or party-coded framing ("liberal," "conservative," "Republican," "Democrat").
- No anti-technology framing ("the data center monster," "Big Tech," "Silicon Valley as a villain").
- No legal accusations unsupported by a public document. Use public-record language: "public records show," "the current record raises questions," "records requested," "needs verification."
- No claim that anyone broke the law unless counsel has reviewed and approved that claim.
- No language that promises a specific outcome ("we will defeat this"). Promises set the coalition up to be wrong on the record.

If a sentence violates any of the above, rewrite it. There is no margin for these.

## Body register, in detail

- Declarative. State what is true.
- No loaded adjectives in body prose. "Massive," "shocking," "unprecedented," "horrific," "looming," "ominous" are out. "266-acre," "second-reading," "first-quarter" are in.
- Cite numbers when you have them. Ordinance numbers, dates, gallons, acres, parcel IDs.
- Separate confirmed facts from community concerns. The phrase "residents are asking" is the workhorse here.
- Quote sparingly. When you do, use double quotes and keep the quote short.

Good body copy:
- *Public records show a nearly 267-acre site near Foxfire was rezoned to a Planned Industrial District for data centers.*
- *Residents are asking for site-specific numbers on water demand, cooling design, and fire flow.*
- *Ordinance 2024-07 was adopted on May 20, 2024 by a 4-1 vote.*

Bad body copy:
- *The Village rammed through a massive industrial complex.*
- *Behind closed doors, K-Nova got everything it wanted.*

## Hero and CTA register, in detail

The hero register lets the site say plainly what is happening and what to do, without disguising the seriousness of the moment in procedural language.

Allowed in the hero register, by exception to the body's adjective ban:
- "Giant data centers" (descriptive of scale, anchored to the 267-acre figure).
- "Your new neighbor."
- "We can still stop this."
- "Help protect Foxfire."
- "Fight for Foxfire."
- "This is not over."
- "Show up. Speak out."

Still not allowed, even in the hero:
- Personal attacks on named individuals.
- Conspiratorial language.
- Partisan language.
- Promises of legal victory.
- Exclamation points.
- Em dashes.
- Anti-technology framing.

Good hero copy (cleared for use):
- *Giant data centers. Nearly 267 acres. Your new neighbor.*
- *Public records. Public hearings. Public land.*
- *Read the ordinance. Then decide.*
- *This is not over.*

Good CTA copy:
- *Sign up for updates.*
- *Join the Foxfire updates list.*
- *View the timeline.*
- *File a records request.*
- *Email the council.*
- *Help protect Foxfire.*

Bad hero copy:
- *AWS is destroying our neighborhood!* (exclamation, accusation)
- *The Village broke the law.* (legal accusation)
- *Big Tech is coming for your home.* (anti-technology framing)

## The lexicon

Avoid these words and phrases anywhere on the site or in print materials, regardless of register:

- corruption
- illegal
- secret deal
- they lied
- guaranteed health damage
- property values will collapse
- AWS is definitely behind it
- the Village broke the law

Use these instead, from the public-record register:

- public records show
- residents are asking
- the current record raises questions
- this needs independent review
- the coalition is requesting records
- this remains unverified
- confirmed by public records
- community concern
- records requested
- needs verification

Both lists travel together. When unsure which side of the line a phrase falls on, default to the public-record register.

## Buttons

Verbs in sentence case. End with a period when the button stands as a complete sentence.

- *Sign up for updates.*
- *Read the ordinance.*
- *File a records request.*
- *See the timeline.*
- *Open PDF →*

Use the right arrow `→` (literal Unicode arrow, mono-typeset) for forward navigation. Do not use `>` or `>>`.

## Empty states

Name what is missing and what to do next. Two sentences, max.

Good: *No hearing scheduled yet. Check back, or subscribe for updates.*
Good: *No documents filed for this parcel. The next planning packet is expected on April 8.*
Bad: *Nothing to see here.*
Bad: *Coming soon!*

## Numerical formatting

Numbers carry the brand's authority. Typeset them like evidence.

- Ordinance, parcel, and request IDs in IBM Plex Mono: `ORD 2024-07`, `FXF-PR-2026-001`, `266.971 ac`.
- Dates as `MM.DD` over the year: `04.08 / 2026`. (Component already does this; do not retype.)
- Gallons and acres get a unit, no comma decoration: `1,250,000 gal/day`, `266.971 ac`.
- Tabular numerals on every table cell, every timeline date, every doc card date.
- Never italicize numbers for emphasis.

## Acceptable taglines

These have been cleared for use anywhere, masthead, social, signoff:

- Public records. Public hearings. Public land.
- Read the ordinance. Then decide.
- Civic. Procedural. Rooted in place.
- This is not over.
- Help protect Foxfire.

If you want a new one, run it through the do/don't list before using it. Hero replacements are allowed; body copy stays in the procedural register.

## Things never to write

- Anything alarmist or apocalyptic ("the data center will destroy our community").
- Anything conspiratorial.
- Anything partisan or party-coded.
- Anything snarky, sarcastic, or aggrieved.
- Anti-technology language.
- Personal attacks on named individuals or companies.
- Legal conclusions ("the Village broke the law," "this was illegal") not reviewed by counsel.

If a sentence sounds like it could appear in a campaign mailer or a tech-startup landing page, rewrite it.

## When in doubt

The voice is **factual, procedural, calm, specific** in the body, and **direct, plain, firm** in the hero and the CTAs. If you are uncertain whether a line clears the bar, drop it. The coalition has time. The site is on the record.
