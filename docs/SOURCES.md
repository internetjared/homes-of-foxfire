# Sources

The coalition's credibility is built on the public record. Every factual claim that ships on this site has a publicly verifiable source. This file is the standard.

## The rule, in one line

If you cannot cite it, you cannot ship it. There is no exception for "everyone knows this."

## What counts as a source

Sources are ranked by how easily a reader can verify them. Use the highest-ranked source available for each claim.

1. **Primary government records.** Adopted ordinances, resolutions, council and committee meeting minutes, public hearing minutes, agendas, planning commission filings, county auditor parcel records, county recorder deeds, Secretary of State business filings, court dockets, state statutes by section number. These are the strongest sources. Most claims on this site should cite one of these.

2. **Government correspondence and packets obtained through public records requests.** Acceptable when archived with a clear chain of custody. Cite the records request ID (`FXF-PR-2026-NNN`), the date received, and a link to the archived copy.

3. **Peer-reviewed research and authoritative national reports.** For data center water use, electricity demand, cooling design, and economic impact framing, prefer Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, Uptime Institute, the Virginia JLARC report, the U.S. Energy Information Administration, and the Ohio EPA Division of Air Pollution Control. Note the publishing organization in the source label.

4. **Mainstream press, on the record.** Acceptable for color and context, but a press article should not be the only source for a procedural or legal claim.

5. **Public-meeting statements by named individuals.** Acceptable to report what someone said at a meeting, as long as the citation makes clear that this is a meeting statement, not an established fact. Use phrasing like "according to the February 23, 2026 council minutes" or "as stated by Mayor Geiger on February 23, 2026."

The following are not acceptable as sources for public claims:

- Anonymous tips.
- Social media posts unless the author is the named source and the post is the official record (a council member's official channel, a verified company account).
- Unverified resident statements presented as fact about other people.
- Earlier coalition copy. The coalition's own pages cannot be the source for the coalition's other pages.

## How to cite

Every source on the site appears as a labeled link, not a bare URL. The label is the document's actual name, not the filename or the slug.

Good: `[Ordinance 2024-07 (PDF)](https://www.commercialpointohio.gov/CPDocuments/CPOrdinances/2024/2024-07.pdf)`

Bad: `[Click here](https://www.commercialpointohio.gov/CPDocuments/CPOrdinances/2024/2024-07.pdf)`

Bad: `(2024-07.pdf)`

Pages that assert multiple facts end with a "Sources" block. Inline citations should not interrupt the prose; place the link at the end of the sentence or in a sources block.

## Verifying a source before publishing

Before any new claim ships, the writer:

1. Opens the source URL and confirms the document still exists at that URL.
2. Confirms the cited fact appears in the document. If the fact comes from a long PDF, note the page number in the data file's `body` or in a sentence in the page text.
3. Saves a copy of the document in the coalition Google Drive in the appropriate folder. The repo links to the village or county URL; the Drive carries the archive copy.
4. If the document moves or disappears, the source field gets updated with a `web.archive.org` URL.

## Confidence levels

When the public record does not yet support a claim cleanly, the page must say so. Use these three levels.

- **Confirmed.** A primary or secondary source supports the claim. No qualifier needed.
- **Reported.** A statement was made on the public record by a named individual but has not yet been independently verified. Use phrasing like "according to," "as stated by," or "as reflected in the February 23, 2026 minutes."
- **Unverified.** The coalition is researching the question but cannot yet support a claim. Do not publish unverified claims as fact. If a question is unverified and important, publish the question on the page with a note that records have been requested.

## Disclaimer language

Every page that touches legal or procedural matters carries this disclaimer in the footer block of the page or section:

> The Homes of Foxfire Coalition is a volunteer neighborhood organization. Nothing on this site is legal advice. Residents who believe they have a legal interest in any matter described here should consult a licensed attorney.

For pages that touch health, environmental, or utility-impact matters, this companion line is added:

> Health, environmental, and utility figures cited here come from public sources. They are illustrative of the kinds of impacts large data center facilities can produce and are not a prediction about the specific facilities under discussion. Facility-specific impacts depend on facility-specific design, which is not yet on the public record.

## Claims that cannot ship without counsel review

The following narratives have legal sensitivity and must be reviewed by an Ohio land-use, municipal, or consumer-protection attorney before they appear on the public site.

- Any claim that an ordinance was adopted improperly under Ohio Revised Code 731.17, 731.30, or related provisions.
- Any claim that the M/I Homes sales process failed disclosure obligations to homebuyers.
- Any claim of conflict of interest or self-dealing by a named council member, official, or developer.
- Any allegation that a private entity violated state or federal law.

The procedural history of Ordinance 2024-07 may be reported as facts (date adopted, vote count, named voters, suspension-of-readings vote count if recorded) without counsel review, because those are matters of public record. Characterizing those facts as "illegal" or "improper" is not allowed without counsel review.

## When in doubt

Ship less. A page that says "we are researching" with a list of records requests in flight is a stronger civic document than a page that says "we believe" with no source. The coalition's leverage is the public record, not rhetoric.
