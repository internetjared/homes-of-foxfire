## The story in one paragraph

In May 2024, the Village of Commercial Point rezoned approximately 266.971 acres directly north of the Foxfire neighborhood from Exceptional Use to Planned Industrial District, with development standards permitting only data centers. The rezoning (Ordinance 2024-07) was adopted under emergency procedure on a single night, by a 4-1 vote, after a public-hearing process most residents say they never received notice of. The ordinance was preceded by an annexation in 2023, a code amendment removing the one-year refiling bar two weeks before adoption, and a 48-hour resubmission waiver granted under undisclosed "extenuating circumstances." On May 4, 2026, the Village Council adopted Ordinance 2026-09, an 18-month moratorium on new data center development. The 2024 rezoning still stands. The procedural-defect window under ORC 713.121 closes around May 20, 2026.

## Timeline (chronological, all events anchored to public record)

### Pre-2023 — first interest
- **2021-05-17** — First K-Nova rezoning hearing on Jahn Farm parcel. Residents raised traffic, property value, wildlife, and rural-character concerns. Attorney delivered 200+ signatures in opposition.
- **2021-11-15** — Follow-up K-Nova hearing on revised plans and landscaping. Discussion of mounds, fencing, traffic lights, truck traffic, lighting, diesel smell.

### 2023 — annexation and code prep
- **2023-05-30** — Pickaway County records Jahn / K-Nova annexation petition (~267.59 ac). Petitioners: Eric Young as Trustee, Alice Jahn, K-Nova LLC. Agent: Craig Moncrief, Plank Law Firm.
- **2023-06-05** — Village adopts Resolution 17-2023, statement of services for the annexation. Notably stated the village did not then have sanitary sewer capacity and only estimated capacity within two years.
- **2023-06-27** — Pickaway County approves the annexation petition. References parcel L2700010053101.
- **2023-07** — Amazon Data Services NDA signed by previous Mayor Goldhardt. Three-year term, auto-renews July 2026 absent 90-day notice.
- **2023-08-07** — Ordinance 2023-09 amends Chapter 1171.02(c), adding "data centers" as permitted use in Limited Manufacturing District. Declared an emergency. First village action expressly making data centers a permitted use.
- **2023-10-02** — Ordinance 2023-10 amends Section 1042.05 to allow Village Administrator to issue permits for discharge of cooling water and unpolluted industrial process water into sanitary sewer.
- **2023-10-16** — Committee of the Whole. Bruce Moore (K-Nova attorney) tells council the Jahn property would be rezoned PID like the property to the north and that K-Nova had been receiving inquiries from data centers. Says he might ask council to waive the third reading and wants the property rezoned by year-end.
- **2023-11-06** — Council. Dustyn Fox asks whether the village aquifer can supply 5 million additional gallons per day and what South Central would need to supply 50 additional megawatts. References someone whose company represents "a large tech company in Ohio" who declined to speak because of an NDA.
- **2023-12-04** — Ordinance 2023-11 annexes 267.59 acres into Commercial Point.
- **2023-12-04** — Resolution 38-2023 accepts limited warranty deed for ~4.308 acres from K-Nova LLC for ROW use on SR 104 / Durrett Rd. Emergency clause states the real estate closing between K-Nova and its "potential buyer" needed to be accomplished as soon as possible.

### 2024 — the procedural-defect window
- **2024-01-05** — K-Nova files first 2024 rezoning application (266.971 acres, Exceptional Use → Planned Industrial District). Becomes Ordinance 2024-02.
- **2024-02-26** — Public hearing on first K-Nova rezoning. M/I Homes' Josh Barkan tells council M/I wants to maintain a residential environment for Foxfire and had been talking with K-Nova about mounds, buffering, landscaping along southern border. Residents raised health, environment, noise, light, air, public-vote, police-coverage, and electrical-substation concerns.
- **2024-03-18** — Committee of the Whole. Jay Weaver tells council Bruce Moore had been contacting council members individually. Mayor Goldhardt agrees the developer was "skirting around the Sunshine Law." Solicitor Mattes warns that personal-device emails and texts about village business are public records. Same meeting includes AWS-property TIF discussion: K-Nova would still collect generated TIF money even if AWS, as a new parcel owner, activated the TIF.
- **2024-04-01** — On its third reading, Ordinance 2024-02 receives no motion to adopt. A separate motion to amend exhibits dies for lack of a second. Public speakers: Ron Jahn, Eric Young, Josh Barkan (M/I), Bruce Moore (K-Nova). Bruce Moore states the property would be used for data centers only, no warehouses.
- **2024-04-15** — Special Council Meeting. Council waives the one-year refiling rule on the condition that K-Nova refile within 48 hours, citing "extenuating circumstances." The public record does not state what those circumstances were.
- **2024-04-16** — K-Nova files second 2024 rezoning application within 48 hours of waiver. Becomes Ordinance 2024-07.
- **2024-05-06** — Council adopts Ordinance 2024-05 amending Chapter 1143.04(e). Resubmission bar shortened from one year to six months. Council action window changed from 45 days to 120 days. Council Member Laura Wolfe's resignation effective same day.
- **2024-05-20** — Special Council adopts Ordinance 2024-07 by 4-1 vote. Yes: Geiger, Nungester, Weaver, Ratliff. No: Crego. Both a vote on suspension of readings and a separate vote on passage. Standards permit data centers as the only use on the property and allow utilities to be aboveground where absolutely necessary. Public hearing held same evening lists four members present (Geiger, Weaver, Nungester, Crego), an attendance discrepancy that the special-meeting record will need to resolve.

### 2026 — site plan and moratorium
- **2026-02-02** — Council approves a Major Site Plan filed by Scannell Properties #627 LLC on behalf of K-Nova. Bruce Moore says K-Nova "may or may not" return for a CRA. Bill Scala says K-Nova will return to ask for the CRA amendment. Wastewater plant capacity confirmed at 1.4 MGD; current flows 400-500K GPD.
- **2026-02-23** — Council. Council Member Miller submits proposed data-center moratorium resolution. Resident Corey Wasmus raises the AWS / Amazon Data Services NDA, signed July 2023 by previous mayor with auto-renewal July 2026. Mayor Geiger says she sent non-renewal notice. Council Member Nungester states the Durrett Road site allows data centers "by right." Same evening, Ordinance 2026-03 prohibits gas-fired electric generation facilities using turbines in all zoning districts.
- **2026-03-16** — Committee of the Whole. Miller's emergency moratorium resolution (then a 180-day version) taken up. Some community members ask for a one-year moratorium. K-Nova representative Bill Scala addresses Council and signals possible legal action if Council moves in a different direction (YouTube video h-iF_kd2qUA, timestamp 2:02:22).
- **2026-03-31** — Major Site Plan K-Nova II submitted by Scannell Properties #627 LLC for the 266.971 acre parcel. Eight 220,000-sq-ft buildings, a 45,000-sq-ft substation support building, an electrical substation, a water tower, a security building, and seven stormwater basins.
- **2026-04-06** — Council Meeting. Press coverage the following day (Scioto Post, April 7) reports Commercial Point preparing for legal fight, advancing ordinance opposition, and exploring legal avenues to challenge the K-Nova / data-center project. Quote attributed to Council Member Miller: "This first step may well result in litigation, but we must protect the soul of our community."
- **2026-04-20** — Special Council agenda lists Ordinance 2026-09 as an 18-month moratorium on data center development. Same agenda also includes proposed comprehensive plan contract, proposed planning commission ordinance, and "AI Medical Imaging Processing Campus" item. Committee of the Whole same evening considers two proposed transparency measures (development registry; mandatory early public disclosure for major development proposals) and a K-Nova major site plan discussion.
- **2026-04-29** — Village Zoning Administrator Jim Kuzelka denies K-Nova II Major Site Plan as incomplete under Codified Ordinance 1141.05, citing nine specific deficiencies: unsigned/unsealed surveys; inaccurate adjoining-property descriptions; missing setback lines along AWS-owned property to north; missing fencing details; missing floor counts and building heights; missing elevations for buildings 1-8; no proposed refuse-removal pad location; missing recreation/open-space sizing; no easements or rights-of-way on proposed site. Letter references K-Nova II's "current threat of litigation" and routes further communication through Solicitor Mattes. Delivered by email to Bill Scala, Bruce Moore, Bill Mattes.
- **2026-05-04** — Council adopts Ordinance 2026-09 by 5-1 vote. Council Member Nungester is the only no vote. Many community members spoke at the meeting before the vote. The 18-month moratorium takes effect immediately.

9. **Sunshine Law (March 18, 2024).** What individual contacts did Bruce Moore make with council members? Were any village-business communications conducted on personal devices?

## Comparable cases (Ohio + national)

- **Wake Up JeffCo** (Jefferson County, OH) — coalition lawsuit against data center development. Coalition has reached out and pulled their complaint.
- **Birch Solar** (Allen County, OH, 2024) — initially blocked by local opposition; Ohio Supreme Court later ruled OPSB rejection was improper.
- **Prince William County / Digital Gateway** (Virginia) — massive data center project; opposition; court rulings; project ultimately abandoned when county leadership chose not to appeal further.
- **Virginia JLARC report** — found data centers can generate substantial local tax revenue and high-paying operating jobs, but the largest economic benefit usually arrives during construction. Typical 250,000 sq ft data center has roughly 50 full-time staff (about half contractors); peak construction employment can be ~1,500 for a single building.
- **Maine** (April 2026) — Legislature sent a bill to the governor barring permits for data centers ≥20 MW until November 1, 2027.
- **Bangor, ME** — adopted six-month pause.
- **Ashville, OH** (April 2026) — village council approved a $102M EdgeConneX data center, contrasting with Commercial Point.
- **Ohio statewide** — proposed 2026 ballot amendment ("Ohio Prohibition of Data Center Construction Amendment") would ban large-scale data center construction.
- **Ord 2026-09 federal authority comparison** — the moratorium cites ORC 715.01 and 713.02 as police-power authority, consistent with how local moratoriums have been upheld where they create planning time without effecting a regulatory taking.

## Brand voice and style

The coalition operates in two registers, both bound by hard rules.

**Body register (default).** Factual, procedural, calm, specific. Cite numbers. Separate confirmed facts from community concerns. Use "public records show," "residents are asking," "needs verification."

**Hero/CTA register (sparingly).** Direct, plain, firm. Allowed phrases: "Giant data centers," "Your new neighbor," "We can still stop this," "Help protect Foxfire," "This is not over."

**Hard rules (both registers):**
- **No em dashes.** Use a period, a comma, or a colon.
- **No exclamation points anywhere.**
- **No conspiratorial language** ("rigged," "secret deal," "they don't want you to know").
- **No partisan or party-coded framing.**
- **No anti-technology framing** ("Big Tech as villain," "the data center monster").
- **No legal accusations unsupported by a public document or counsel review.**
- **No promises of specific legal or political outcomes.**
- Numbers use IBM Plex Mono with tabular figures: ORD 2024-07, FXF-PR-2026-001, 266.971 ac.
- Sentence case for headings and buttons. Buttons end with a period when they stand as a complete sentence.
- The right arrow is the literal Unicode → not > or >>.

**Lexicon — phrases to use:** "public records show," "residents are asking," "needs verification," "the questions on the timeline are still open," "the work continues."

**Lexicon — phrases to avoid:** "We will stop this," "obviously corrupt," "Big Tech," "our enemies on council," "they're hiding the truth," "this is rigged."
